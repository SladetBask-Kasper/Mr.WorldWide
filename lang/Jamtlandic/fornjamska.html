Transliteration of "Frösöstenen/Frösöystein"

AustmaÞr GuÞfastar Sun lit rai(sa stein) Þina aukirua bru Þisauk han lit kristna Iatalant Asbiurn kirÞi bru triun raist äuk tsain runar Þisar

Translation of "Frösöstenen/Frösöystein"

Modern Swedish literal translation

Austmthr Guthfastar Sun lät resa denna sten och göra denna bro och han lät kristna Iatalant Asbiurn gjorde bron Triun ristade och Tsain dessa runor.

Modern Swedish translation

Östman Gudfast Son lät resa denna sten, göra denna bro och han lät kristna Jämtland. Åsbjörn gjorde bron. Triun och Tsain (Sten?) ristade dessa runor.

English translation

Östman Gudfast Son (The man from the east, son of the godfearing one) let raise this stone, make this bridge and he let Jamtland become Christian. Åsbjörn (The ridge bear) built the bridge. Triun and Tsain carved these runes.

Notes

In this text we see a couple of interesting things about how it is written. Firstly we have three runes which aren't too common among Swedish rune stones. We also have an interesting word for "Jämtland". So the name "Jämtland" has three versions. The Jamtlandic/Old-Norwegian "Jamtland/Jamtaland/Jamtlänn", the Danish "Jemtland" and the Swedish "Jämtland". Here we see an early version of what was to become "Jamtaland", namely "Iatalant", which doesn't have the "m".

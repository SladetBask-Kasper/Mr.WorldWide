Yiddish - Main page

Yiddish is a germanic language which has loanwords from a little bit everywhere. An incomplete list of languages they have taken words from would look a little like this: Hebrew, Aramaic, Spanish, French, German, English, Polish, Ukrainian and Russian. Words from the sea to North Africa, to the Russian border with North Korea.

The place where the lanugage developed the most is in Germany and this is is no suprise that the language sounds a lot like german. If you already speak german then learning yiddish will be really easy. The language largly developed in so-called "Juden Stra√üe" or ghettos, where almost all jewish people lived and where it wasn't too uncommon to never leave the ghetto. Not even for a walk. It was largly around the Rhein river it developed. Thus the yiddish language is alot like the german spoken around the Rhein.

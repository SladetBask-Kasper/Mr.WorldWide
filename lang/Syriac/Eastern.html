Syriac Alphabet (ܐܠܦ ܒܝܬ ܣܘܪܝܝܐ - Alef bet Suryaya)

Is the Syriac alphabet hard to learn?

Probably not. I've left the Hebrew letter along with the roman one down below to make it easier to learn for people who already know at least the Hebrew alphabet. I imagine if you're trying to learn Syriac you're probably a Jew or a Christian who already speaks a little Hebrew. There might be some Arabic speakers who are reading this, there is a version with arabic equivilant characters over at the "Aramaic" page. For those of you who don't know any semitic alphabet, it might be a bit tricky to get the hang of things.

What does "Alef bet" mean?

So "Alef bet" is just the name of the first two letters of the alphabet. Just like our word in english "Alphabet" is just the name of the first to letters of the Greek alphabet (which the roman one is based upon): Alpha Beta.
